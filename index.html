Vision and Mission

Vision: A centre of excellence in providing human rights-based correctional services in Africa.

Mission: To contribute to the protection and development of society by providing safe, secure, and humane custody of offenders while placing human rights at the centre of their correctional programmes.

Officer in Charge Statement

The Officer in Charge (OC) is responsible for maintaining order, discipline, and the safe custody of prisoners.

Core Values

  1. Professionalism: We shall adhere to the professional codes of conduct, exhibit a high degree of competence, and follow best practices.
  2. Reliability: We shall be accountable, trustworthy, and efficient in conducting our work.
  3. Integrity: We shall uphold our ethical values, serve with honesty, and maintain zero tolerance for corruption.
  4. Security: We shall ensure the safety and security of our clients and the property of the prisons at all times.
  5. Oneness: We shall exhibit unity of purpose in all aspects of prison work.
  6. Nationalistic: We shall be nationalistic in outlook, character, and practices.
  7. Discipline: We shall always behave in a manner that conforms with the rules and regulations.

Rehabilitation Area

Formal Education

The rehabilitation of inmates at the UG Prisons Farm Kitalya through a formal education program offers a transformative opportunity for personal growth and reintegration into society. By providing access to structured learning, this initiative equips prisoners with essential academic and vocational skills, fostering a sense of purpose and self-worth. Formal education not only empowers the individuals within the prison system but also lays the groundwork for reducing recidivism, as educated individuals are more likely to secure stable employment upon release and reintegrate successfully into their communities. Moreover, the program helps shift the focus from punitive measures to rehabilitative strategies, offering a chance for inmates to improve their lives through intellectual and emotional development.

By providing access to structured learning, this initiative equips prisoners with essential academic and vocational skills, fostering a sense of purpose and self-worth.

Vocational

The vocational training program at UG Prisons Farm Kitalya provides inmates with practical skills that enhance their employability and foster self-reliance. Courses offered include carpentry and joinery, metal works and fabrication, tailoring, salon services, shoe making, polishing and shining, soap making, agriculture and crop husbandry, as well as crafts making.

These hands-on training opportunities equip inmates with a diverse range of skills that not only enable them to contribute meaningfully within the prison but also prepare them for successful reintegration into society. By learning trades that are in demand, inmates gain the confidence and qualifications to pursue stable livelihoods upon release, helping to break the cycle of recidivism and promoting long-term rehabilitation.

Other Trainings

In addition to vocational education, UG Prisons Farm Kitalya provides a range of other training programs aimed at addressing the diverse needs of inmates and supporting their rehabilitation journey. Life skills training focuses on equipping inmates with essential skills for personal development, such as effective communication, decision-making, stress management, and financial literacy. These skills help prepare inmates for reintegration into society, fostering emotional resilience and better relationships. The elderly inmates’ intervention program specifically caters to the older population, providing tailored support in health care, social interaction, and training opportunities that align with their needs and abilities.

Another significant initiative is The Prisoner’s Journey (TPJ), a program by Prison Fellowship Uganda that focuses on spiritual rehabilitation. This transformative course helps inmates explore faith and forgiveness, guiding them through a process of personal reflection and healing. Discipleship training, through ministry programs, offers inmates the chance to deepen their spiritual lives and gain a sense of purpose, hope, and inner peace. These combined training efforts not only equip inmates with practical skills for the future but also help them rebuild their lives emotionally, mentally, and spiritually, providing them with the tools necessary to reintegrate as productive, positive members of society.

Other Rehabilitation Activities

Comprehensive Rehabilitation Programs: UG Prisons Farm Kitalya recognizes the importance of mental, emotional, and social well-being in the rehabilitation process. Through structured counseling and guidance sessions, inmates are empowered to overcome personal challenges and emotional barriers. Spiritual therapy plays a pivotal role, fostering inner peace and a sense of purpose by encouraging inmates to explore faith-based healing. In addition, the facility promotes physical wellness and team spirit through sports and games, while Music, Dance, and Drama (MDD) serve as creative outlets for self-expression and personal growth. By integrating these activities with modern technologies such as active TV and personal radios, inmates remain connected to the world outside, enhancing their rehabilitation journey. The program also ensures access to justice, providing inmates with the opportunity to seek legal advice and resources, while patriotism training and clubs nurture a sense of national pride, reinforcing the importance of community and societal responsibility.

Holistic Rehabilitation Initiatives: At UG Prisons Farm Kitalya, the rehabilitation approach extends beyond traditional methods to include multifaceted initiatives aimed at fostering holistic recovery. Inmates benefit from personalized counseling and guidance, helping them address past traumas and cultivate emotional resilience. Spiritual therapy is integrated into this framework, offering inmates a deeper sense of connection and personal transformation. Furthermore, the facility encourages physical engagement through sports and games, allowing inmates to develop teamwork and discipline. Music, Dance, and Drama (MDD) programs are instrumental in providing an artistic space for inmates to express themselves and build confidence. Active TV and personal radios serve as vital links to the outside world, keeping inmates informed and connected. The facility also prioritizes access to justice, ensuring inmates receive adequate legal support, while patriotism training and clubs inspire a strong sense of duty and love for the country, contributing to personal reform and reintegration into society.

Social Reintegration

Social Reintegration Services: UG Prisons Farm Kitalya endeavors to facilitate the seamless social reintegration of inmates by offering specialized services on a quarterly basis. These services are meticulously structured to address the holistic needs of individuals, focusing on skill acquisition, educational advancement, and vocational training that align with post-release opportunities. The reintegration package extends beyond the institutional setting, incorporating mentorship, community linkage, and tailored support systems that aid in the rehabilitation process. These initiatives are designed to empower inmates, fostering their self-sufficiency and resilience, thereby equipping them to reintegrate effectively into society. The facility’s forward-thinking approach ensures that every aspect of an inmate’s reintegration is strategically planned and personalized to meet both societal expectations and individual aspirations.

Follow-up, Aftercare, and Human Rights Commitment: The facility places a strong emphasis on follow-up and aftercare, ensuring that reintegrated individuals receive continuous support even after their release. A robust aftercare program is in place to address the various challenges faced by former inmates, from employment facilitation to ongoing counseling, ensuring that they remain positively engaged within their communities. This extended care not only reduces the likelihood of recidivism but also fosters a supportive environment for personal growth. Furthermore, the institution upholds an unwavering commitment to human rights observance, ensuring that every individual, during their incarceration and reintegration, is treated with the utmost dignity, fairness, and respect. This holistic approach is foundational in guaranteeing that the rights of inmates are safeguarded, with an emphasis on restorative justice that supports long-term societal harmony.
