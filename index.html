Prisons Act

The Prisons Act of Uganda provides the legal framework for the operations of the Uganda Prisons Service. It outlines the roles, responsibilities, and powers of prison officers, as well as the rights of prisoners. The act serves as a cornerstone for the rehabilitation and management of prisoners in the country.

Vision and Mission

Vision: A centre of excellence in providing human rights-based correctional services in Africa.
Mission: To contribute to the protection and development of society by providing safe, secure, and humane custody of offenders while placing human rights at the centre of their correctional programmes.

Officer in Charge Statement

The Officer in Charge (OC) is responsible for maintaining order, discipline, and the safe custody of prisoners. The OC is also instrumental in driving the reforms within the prison system, ensuring that inmates are rehabilitated and reintegrated into society as law-abiding citizens.

Core Values

Professionalism: We shall adhere to professional codes of conduct, exhibit a high degree of competence, and implement best practices.
Reliability: We shall be accountable, trustworthy, effective, and efficient in conducting our work.
Integrity: We shall uphold ethical values and maintain zero tolerance for corruption.
Security: We shall ensure the safety and security of our clients and property at all times.
Oneness: We shall exhibit unity of purpose in all aspects of our work.
Nationalistic: We shall be nationalistic in outlook, character, and practice.
Discipline: We shall adhere to rules, regulations, and codes of conduct and ethics.

Mr. Andrew Kenneth Byekwaso

Superintendent of Prisons (S.P.)

Mr. Andrew Kenneth Byekwaso is a consummate leader whose strategic foresight and exceptional problem-solving acumen have earned him the respect of both his colleagues and the broader community. His leadership is characterized by a methodical approach to complex challenges, consistently employing analytical rigor and innovative thinking to enhance the operational efficiency of the prison facility. By fostering a culture of continuous improvement, Mr. Byekwaso has successfully integrated modern technologies and systems that not only bolster security but also optimize agricultural production, making the farm an exemplary model of self-sustainability. His decision-making processes are rooted in a balanced consideration of both short-term operational needs and long-term vision, ensuring that each challenge is met with a solution that is both pragmatic and forward-thinking.Beyond his technical expertise, Mr. Byekwaso has cultivated a harmonious and highly productive working environment through his unwavering commitment to nurturing positive relationships with his staff. He recognizes that effective leadership hinges on empowering individuals, and as such, he emphasizes transparency, open communication, and professional development. This inclusive management style has fostered a collaborative atmosphere where staff feel valued and motivated to contribute to the facility's success. His deep passion for the Uganda Prisons Service is evident in his tireless dedication to its mission, and his genuine love for the department drives him to continuously seek innovative ways to elevate the work of his team, instilling a sense of pride and purpose across all levels of operation.
