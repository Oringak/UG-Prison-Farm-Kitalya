Commissioner General of Prisons

Canon Dr. Johnson Omuhunde Rwashote Byabashaija

Canon. Dr. Johnson Omuhunde Rwashote Byabashaija is a distinguished Ugandan civil servant, veterinary doctor, and dedicated leader who has served as the Commissioner General of Uganda Prisons Service since 2005. His leadership and commitment to improving Uganda’s prison system have made him a respected figure in public service and the Church of Uganda North Kigezi.

Early Life and Education

Born on 27th September 1957 in Kajumiro village, Nyakishenyi sub-county, Rubabo County, Rukungiri District, Dr. Byabashaija is the first-born of seven children. His education began at Nyakishenyi Primary School, continuing through Kamwezi Primary School, Mukyayi Primary School, and Mutorere Secondary School. He later attended Makerere College School for A-level studies and Makerere University, where he earned a degree in Veterinary Medicine. In 1986, he obtained a Master of Science degree from the University of Glasgow.

Career

Dr. Byabashaija’s career began as a teacher before joining the Uganda Prisons Service in 1982. Over the years, he rose through the ranks, becoming the Commissioner General of Uganda Prisons in 2005. Under his leadership, the Uganda Prisons Service has undergone significant reforms, including:

  • Modernizing prison infrastructure.
  • Implementing innovative rehabilitation programs to reduce recidivism.
  • Enhancing security and welfare for inmates and staff.

Achievements

During his tenure, Dr. Byabashaija has been recognized for his leadership and numerous contributions:

  • Significant reforms in prison management and rehabilitation.
  • Strengthened security protocols within Uganda's prisons.
  • Contributions to the local agricultural economy through poultry farming.

Personal Life

Dr. Byabashaija is married to Mrs. Janipher Byabashaija and is a dedicated family man. He serves as a Lay Canon in the Church of Uganda North Kigezi, reflecting his deep spiritual values and commitment to justice and compassion.
